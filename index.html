J. R. R. Tolkien

John Ronald Reuel Tolkien was an English writer, poet, philologist, and academic, best known as the author of the high fantasy works The Hobbit and The Lord of the Rings.

Here's a time line of John Ronald Reuel Tolkien:

  • 1892 - Birth of John Ronald Reuel Tolkien in Bloemfontein, Orange Free State (now part of South Africa).
  • 1896 - Death of Arthur Reuel Tolkien, Tolkien’s father, in Bloemfontein.
  • 1904 - Death of Mabel Suffield, Tolkien’s mother.
  • 1905 - Aunt Beatrice accepts, him and his brother Hilary, into her home in Stirling Road.
  • 1907 - Tolkien invents his private language ‘Naffarin’ based on Spanish and Latin.
  • 1909 - The boys move to Duchess Road and live with Mrs. Faulkner. Tolkien meets Edith Bratt, another lodger.
  • 1910 - Ronald’s romance with Edith continues until Father Francis forbids him to communicate with her.
  • 1910 - Edith moves to Cheltenham.
  • 1910 - Tolkien obtains a scholarship at Oxford’s Exeter College.
  • 1911 - Tolkien’s poem ‘The Battle of the Eastern Field’ is printed in the King Edward’s School Chronicle.
  • 1911 - Tolkien begins studying at Oxford, studying Finnish.
  • 1913 - Tolkien writes to Edith on his 21st birthday, and soon after they become engaged.
  • 1914 - Tolkien writes his first identifiable “Middle-earth” fragment ‘The Voyage of Éarendel the Evening Star’.
  • 1916 - Ronald and Edith are married in Warwick.
  • 1916 - Tolkien travels to France. On the boat he writes the poem ‘The Lonely Isle’. Tolkien becomes a Battalion Signalling Officer.
  • 1916 - Battle of the Somme begins. Suffering from trench fever, Tolkien returns to England.
  • 1918 - The Tolkiens return to Oxford. Tolkien obtains employment with the New English Dictionary [Oxford English Dictionary]. Tolkien probably begins work on the first version of ‘The Music of the Ainur’.
  • 1920 - He begins work as a Reader in English Language at Leeds University.
  • 1930 - About this time, Tolkien may have written the first sentence of The Hobbit: “In a hole in the ground there lived a hobbit“.
  • 1936 - Beowulf: The Monsters and the Critics is published.
  • 1937 - The Hobbit is published. Because of its success, Stanley Unwin subsequently urges Tolkien to write a sequel, which he begins. This is the germ of The Lord of the Rings.
  • 1945 - Tolkien becomes Oxford’s Merton Professor of English Language and Literature.
  • 1949 - Farmer Giles of Ham is published.
  • 1950 - Tolkien gives an ultimatum to Sir Stanley Unwin requesting an immediate response in answer to his suggestion to publish both The Lord of the Rings and The Silmarillion. Later that year Waldman informs Tolkien that The Lord of the Rings must be cut.
  • 1952 - Collins, the publishers, decline to publish The Lord of the Rings.
  • 1953 - Publication of The Homecoming of Beorhtnoth Beorhthelm’s Son.
  • 1954 - Publication of The Fellowship of the Ring.
  • 1953 - Publication of The Two Towers.
  • 1954 - Tolkien wins an International Fantasy Award.
  • 1962 - The Adventures of Tom Bombadil is published.
  • 1971 - Death of Edith Tolkien.
    • 1972 - Tolkien receives his CBE from the Queen at Buckingham Palace.
  • 1973 - Tolkien writes about Galadriel and Celeborn – probably the final addition to his legendarium.
  • 1973 - Death of J.R.R. Tolkien from a stomach ulcer.
